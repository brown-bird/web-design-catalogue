BRUTALISM

THE ANTI-DESIGN DESIGN
60s
1960s ARCHITECTURE → 2010s WEB

Deliberately raw. Confrontationally honest. The ugliness is the point.

ABOUT THIS STYLE

Brutalism takes its name from the béton brut (raw concrete) architecture of the mid-twentieth century. Applied to web design, it emerged as a rejection of the polished, gradient-heavy aesthetic that dominated the 2010s. Where conventional web design hides its structure, brutalism exposes it.

The style is characterized by intentional rawness: thick visible borders, stark monospace type, garish accent colors used without apology, and layouts that reference the blunt grids of early HTML. Sites like Bloomberg Businessweek and Hacker News are touchstones — functional above all else.

Paradoxically, true brutalism requires discipline. Every element must earn its presence, and the aesthetic works only when commitment is total. Half-hearted brutalism just looks broken.
