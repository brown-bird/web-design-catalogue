Glassmorphism

Translucent surfaces, frosted depth, and coloured light bleeding through. Design that exists between the physical and the ethereal.

About This Style

Glassmorphism emerged prominently around 2020–2021, popularized by Apple's macOS Big Sur and Microsoft's Fluent Design. The aesthetic uses backdrop-filter blur to simulate frosted glass — surfaces that are present but see-through, layered over rich, colorful backgrounds.

The technique is technically enabled by CSS backdrop-filter, which applies filters to whatever is behind an element. Combined with semi-transparent backgrounds and refined borders, it creates the illusion of physical depth without heavy drop shadows.

Backgrounds are typically rich, dark gradients punctuated by luminous blobs of color — these "orbs" give glassmorphic surfaces something beautiful to be transparent against. Without a compelling backdrop, the effect falls flat.

The style excels in dashboard UIs, music apps, and any interface where layered depth helps organize information hierarchy. It pairs naturally with soft, rounded components and light, airy typography.
