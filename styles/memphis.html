Memphis
/ 80s

Postmodern rebellion against good taste. Geometric, colorful, irreverent — and absolutely brilliant.

About This Style

The Memphis Group was a Milan-based design collective founded by Ettore Sottsass in 1981. They deliberately violated every principle of "good taste" in design: they used garish color combinations, geometric patterns, cheap laminate materials, and asymmetric forms that made established designers furious.

Their work was a reaction against the functionalist orthodoxy of the 1970s. If modernism said "form follows function," Memphis said "form follows fun." The aesthetic was postmodern before postmodernism had its own name.

Applied to web design, Memphis manifests as polka-dot and zigzag backgrounds, thick black borders with hard box-shadows, a riot of color with no dominant hierarchy, bold geometric decorative elements, and typefaces that feel more like party invitations than professional tools.

CSS techniques: hard offset box-shadows (no blur), border-3px-solid-black everywhere, repeating-linear-gradient for zigzag backgrounds, radial-gradient for dot patterns, and Fredoka One as a rounded but bold display font to carry the playful energy.
