CYBERPUNK

NEON · NOIR · DECAY · DIGITAL OVERLOAD · THE NEAR FUTURE THAT NEVER ARRIVED
// ABOUT THIS STYLE

Cyberpunk visual design draws from the dystopian science fiction of the 1980s and 90s — Gibson's Sprawl, Ridley Scott's Blade Runner, and the neon-soaked rain-slicked streets of an imagined near future. On the web, it translates to dark backgrounds, glowing neon typography, terminal interfaces, and an aesthetic of functional decay.

The palette is anchored by high-voltage cyan, hot pink, and electric yellow against near-black. These are not decorative choices — in cyberpunk, neon is the only light source in a world otherwise dark.

Technical elements — progress bars, system readouts, terminal prompts, grid overlays — are part of the aesthetic vocabulary. Cyberpunk design looks like software that has been running for a decade in a world without polished UX.

CSS techniques: text-shadow for neon glow, repeating-linear-gradient for scanlines, VT323 or Share Tech Mono typefaces, and clip-path animations for glitch effects. The grid background reinforces the digital-space metaphor of early cyber-fiction.
