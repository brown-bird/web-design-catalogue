Neomorphism

Soft, extruded surfaces that feel moulded from the background itself. The illusion of physicality through shadow alone.

About This Style

Neomorphism (or "neumorphism") emerged in late 2019 as a spiritual successor to skeuomorphism, blended with the flat design that replaced it. The technique uses double box-shadows — one light, one dark — applied to elements that share the exact background color, creating an extruded or embossed effect.

The style was popularized by Michal Malewicz's 2019 Dribbble shots and quickly took the design community by storm for its unique physical quality without relying on textures.

Neomorphism has genuine constraints: it works only on a limited range of mid-tone backgrounds (the shadows need contrast in both directions), and accessibility can suffer because the low-contrast approach makes it hard to distinguish interactive from static elements.

Its natural domain is personal apps — music players, health dashboards, smart home controls — where the soft aesthetic creates a calming, tactile experience. It excels in single-purpose screens rather than content-heavy interfaces.
