About This Style

Bento box design takes its name from the traditional Japanese lunch box — a single tray divided into compartments, each holding a different item in perfect proportion. In web design, this translates to a CSS grid of rounded cards at varied sizes, where the asymmetric layout itself communicates hierarchy and emphasis.

Apple popularised the pattern in their product marketing pages — arrays of feature cards where a large card dominates the left or centre, flanked by smaller stat cards, wide horizontal cards for taglines, and accent cells in brand colours. The grid becomes a canvas.

The CSS technique is straightforward: a grid container with named template areas or explicit grid placement, with each card using border-radius: 18px and a minimal box-shadow. Cards are typically white on a light grey background (#F5F5F7 — Apple's surface colour). Dark, blue, and green cells punctuate the grid as accents.

Typography is deliberately content-focused: large numbers, short punchy labels, minimal prose. Inter or system-ui keeps the aesthetic neutral and modern — the layout should do the talking, not the typeface. Interactions are clean hover states (slight scale or shadow lift) rather than dramatic transforms.
