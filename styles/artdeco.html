About This Style

Art Deco flourished between the two World Wars — a celebration of modernity, craftsmanship, and machine-age optimism rendered in geometric ornament. From the Chrysler Building's eagle gargoyles to the Savoy hotel's interior, it stood for glamour achieved through absolute discipline: every decorative element was symmetrical, every line intentional.

The style rejected both Victorian excess and early modernism's severity. Instead it found a middle path — decorative, yes, but decoration governed by mathematical precision. Repeated chevron patterns, sunburst motifs, stepped cornices, and thin gold rule lines became its vocabulary.

Applied to web design, Art Deco translates into near-black backgrounds, gold (#C9A84C) as the exclusive accent, generous use of uppercase spaced lettering, and ornamental border treatments using CSS. Corner brackets made from four short bordered divs replace the carved stone corners of 1930s architecture.

Typography is paired serifs: Playfair Display for display text (dramatic, high-contrast, all-caps) and Cormorant Garamond for body (fine, old-world elegance). Together they create the sense of a Prohibition-era speakeasy membership card or a luxury liner's deck plan — expensive, precise, and unmistakable.
